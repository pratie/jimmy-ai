# ChatDock ideal customer profile

## Primary ICP

Founder-led AI automation, lead-generation, or web agencies that sell website conversion services to appointment- or quote-driven local businesses.

- **Agency stage:** small team, early to growing client roster, and actively productizing an AI chatbot offer
- **Economic buyer:** agency founder, owner, or head of automation
- **Daily user:** founder, implementation specialist, or account manager
- **End clients:** dental and wellness practices, home-service businesses, professional services, fitness, and similar businesses where a qualified inquiry can become an appointment or estimate
- **Core job:** turn a signed client website into a branded, grounded chatbot that answers questions, captures and qualifies leads, and moves the right visitors to a booking
- **Operational job:** manage client agents, conversations, leads, and bookings without rebuilding or monitoring a separate tool stack for every account
- **Commercial job:** show visible outcomes during client reviews so the agency can defend and retain its monthly fee

## Buying triggers

- The agency has signed its first or next chatbot client.
- The agency wants to add AI chatbots to an existing website, SEO, ads, or lead-generation offer.
- Custom builds and disconnected tools are making launches slow or inconsistent.
- The agency needs a working client demo before a proposal or review call.
- The agency is under pressure to prove qualified leads and bookings, not merely that an automation is running.

## Current alternatives

- A custom chatbot build for every client
- A crawler, prompt tool, widget, inbox, spreadsheet, and calendar assembled separately
- A broad CRM or automation suite with a steep setup burden
- Manual screenshots and spreadsheets for client reporting
- A basic website chat widget that answers FAQs but does not support the agency delivery workflow

## Disqualifiers

- A single local business looking only for one self-serve support bot
- Voice-only or outbound-only AI agencies
- Enterprise support teams that require deep ticketing, contact-center, or procurement features
- Agencies whose clients do not depend on inbound website inquiries, qualification, appointments, or estimates
- Buyers seeking mass outbound prospecting rather than inbound website conversion

## Adjacent ICP

Independent web designers, local-business consultants, and fractional marketers who already manage several client websites and want to add a repeatable lead-qualification and booking service.

## Messaging hierarchy

1. **Outcome:** launch client chatbots that qualify visitors and book appointments.
2. **Speed:** move from client website to working demo or launch through one repeatable workflow.
3. **Control:** keep each client’s knowledge, brand, guardrails, and conversations separate.
4. **Retention:** show conversations, captured leads, and bookings instead of reporting that “the bot is live.”
5. **Ease:** avoid rebuilding and babysitting a disconnected stack for every client.

Avoid leading with generic claims such as “AI transformation,” “AI sales team,” or “powerful automation.” Lead with the agency’s client-delivery moment and a measurable visitor outcome.

These signals validate the positioning hypothesis; actual interviews and conversion data should determine whether the primary vertical needs to narrow further.
